## Deck positioning

This deck presents a **product-led, continuous-improvement operating framework** for enterprise innovation in the AI era. It is designed for C-suite and board audiences at life sciences, medtech, health tech, and health systems companies.

**What makes this framework distinctive:**
The dominant consulting frameworks are either process-centric (McKinsey's "Rewired" — six enterprise capabilities), people-centric (BCG's 10-20-70 rule — 70% people and processes), or technology-platform-centric (Microsoft/AWS/Google Cloud Adoption Frameworks). None of them explicitly connects **product-led transformation** — building a solution to galvanize change, then letting processes and resources flow to it — with **continuous improvement methodology** — iterative, evidence-driven cycles applied to innovation management itself. This framework occupies that white space.

**The thesis in one sentence:**
The companies that will win are not the ones that launch the most AI pilots — they are the ones that build a repeatable operating system for turning external technological change into internal competitive advantage.

The Innovation Operating System

**Flywheel center label**
Product-led continuous improvement

**Segment 1 — Sense**

**Segment 2 — Select**
Choose a small number of high-value opportunities. Match each one to buy, partner, invest, or build. Anchor every choice to the core business.

**Principle statement**

**Header data bar**

*Sources: Menlo Ventures 2025 (~500 U.S. decision-makers, 76% purchased); MIT NANDA 2025 (67% vendor vs. 33% internal success rates)*

**Column 1 — Buy**

Watch-outs: Dependency risk, unclear incentives, limited control over roadmap. Partnership value decays if strategic linkage is not maintained.

**Column 3 — Invest**

Best use case: Strategic adjacency, need market visibility or access, want option value without full acquisition or operational commitment.

**Column 4 — Build**

Best use case: Proprietary workflows, unique data advantage, core control points, capabilities tightly linked to competitive edge.

## Appendix: Differentiation notes for speaker preparation

**What this framework is NOT:**
- It is not McKinsey's "Rewired" (six enterprise capabilities, process-centric, top-down)
- It is not BCG's 10-20-70 (people-focused, organizational change management)
- It is not a hyperscaler adoption framework (technology-platform-centric, vendor-aligned)
- It is not Accenture/Deloitte implementation methodology (services-led, execution-focused)

**What this framework IS:**
A product-led, continuous-improvement operating system for innovation. The intellectual foundation blends:

1. **Product-led transformation** (from BCGDV experience): Build a solution first. Put a working product at the center to galvanize change. Processes, resources, and organizational attention flow toward demonstrated value — not top-down mandates. Boomi doubled free-to-paid conversion through a two-week PLG sprint. A vaccine company reached scale "much sooner than initially thought possible" by making the product the organizing principle for cross-functional coordination.

2. **Continuous improvement methodology** (from McKinsey operations experience): Toyota Kata's Improvement Kata — understand the challenge, grasp the current condition, define the next target condition, iterate via rapid experiments. The starting assumption: "We do not know how to meet the challenge because of too much uncertainty, so we have to step our way through it experiment by experiment." Pfizer's Lean Toolbox reduced supply lead times by more than 50%. A pharma company documented 8,000+ problems tackled over three years with 67% of employees solving at least one problem annually.

3. **Applied to AI-era innovation management**: The synthesis is unique. No dominant framework connects product-led adoption with continuous improvement discipline in the context of managing AI innovation portfolios. The 26 of 30 largest pharmaceutical companies that announced major transformations in the last 5 years — with less than one-third achieving sustained performance improvement — are the proof that current approaches are failing.

**The one-line differentiator:**
Other frameworks tell you what to change. This one gives you a repeatable operating system for how to change — continuously, with evidence, anchored by products that demonstrate value before requiring organizational commitment.
